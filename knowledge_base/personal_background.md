# Personal Background & Self-Introduction

## Self-Introduction / Elevator Pitch
I'm a multidisciplinary engineer with a strong foundation in software development, industrial digitalization, AI, and data analytics. I moved to Canada in 2016 and earned my Bachelor of Applied Science in Electrical and Computer Engineering (Minor in Engineering Business) at the University of Toronto. After graduating, I joined Siemens as a Full-Stack Edge Computing Developer, working on factory automation and digital transformation projects—connecting traditional industrial equipment to digital systems so manufacturers could collect, analyze, and act on their operational data. My work spanned MES integration, edge applications, energy management systems, predictive analytics, and industrial IoT.

Those projects taught me that technology alone doesn't create value: data quality, domain expertise, and the ability to turn information into actionable insight are usually what separate success from failure. That insight led me to pursue a Master of Management Analytics at the Rotman School of Management, where I deepened my expertise in data science, machine learning, and business analytics. Today I'm most excited by the intersection of AI, analytics, and software engineering—using technology not just to build systems, but to solve meaningful business problems and deliver measurable impact.

## Why I Moved from Electrical Engineering into Analytics & AI
I don't see it as moving away from engineering—it's continuing to solve the same problems from a deeper perspective. At Siemens, my work centered on industrial digitalization: helping manufacturers connect isolated equipment to digital systems and make operational data accessible and actionable. Across projects I noticed a recurring pattern—organizations rarely lacked software; they lacked high-quality data and people who could extract value from it. I realized that building systems was only half the equation; understanding data, identifying meaningful signals, and translating business challenges into analytical solutions mattered just as much. The rapid rise of large language models and generative AI in 2023 reinforced this: I began to see programming as a powerful tool rather than the end goal. The real value lies in understanding the problem and applying the right technology to solve it. That's why I chose Rotman's MMA program—to combine my engineering foundation, industry experience, and growing interest in AI, and become someone who can bridge technology, data, and business.

## Problems I'm Most Passionate About Solving
I'm drawn to complex, real-world problems at the intersection of technology, data, and business. The most valuable thing engineering gave me wasn't a specific technical skill—it was a structured problem-solving mindset: understand the problem, identify the root cause, evaluate possible solutions, break the problem into manageable components, implement and iterate, then measure results and improve. I'm especially energized by ambiguous problems with no obvious answer. Whether I was connecting industrial equipment to digital systems at Siemens, building analytics solutions, or exploring AI applications today, the underlying challenge is the same: turning complexity into clarity and delivering practical solutions that create measurable value.

## What Differentiates Me
My biggest differentiator is the ability to operate across multiple domains. Compared with a traditional data analyst, I bring a strong engineering and software-development background—I understand how systems are actually built, integrated, and deployed in production. Compared with a traditional software engineer, I have a deeper focus on data, analytics, business impact, and AI-driven decision-making. I also continuously evolve with the technology: over my career I've moved from industrial automation to digital transformation, and from analytics to generative AI, actively seeking to learn and adapt rather than staying within a single discipline. As a result, I can often bridge communication gaps between engineers, business stakeholders, and data scientists, helping teams align around practical solutions and shared goals.

## An Experience That Shaped My Professional Values
One experience left a lasting mark: learning about the Therac-25 incident during my undergraduate studies. My capstone supervisor, Professor Jonathan Rose, shared how a seemingly small software bug in a radiation-therapy machine led to severe injuries and deaths. It fundamentally changed how I think about engineering—engineering isn't just about building technology, it's about responsibility. Today, software systems, data platforms, and AI models influence decisions at scale and affect people's lives, and the consequences of mistakes are very real. The University of Toronto's engineering culture reinforced this; traditions like the Iron Ring ceremony emphasize professional ethics, accountability, and public responsibility. Together these experiences taught me that technical excellence matters, but integrity, rigor, and accountability matter just as much—and they still guide how I work.

## During the Gap Year
After graduating from my master's program in 2024, while waiting for my Canadian permanent residency application to be processed and actively searching for my next opportunity, I used this transition period to build and grow both technically and professionally. During this time, I co-led the development of IDP Plus, a SaaS platform that provides driver's-license translation services for Chinese users who need to rent or drive cars overseas. I worked with partners to turn the idea into a real operating product, covering product design, system architecture, full-stack development, testing, deployment, operations, and business collaboration. At the same time, I systematically studied GenAI and modern AI application development, including Transformer architecture, LLM training, fine-tuning, alignment, RAG, Advanced RAG, Agentic AI, harness design, and loop engineering. I see myself as an early adopter who enjoys exploring emerging technologies early and turning them into practical applications. Although this period was a career transition, I consider it highly valuable. It allowed me to gain hands-on startup experience, strengthen my technical foundation in AI, and develop a stronger ownership mindset as both an engineer and product builder.
