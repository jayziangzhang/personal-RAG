## Livestock Digitalization Project — Context
One of the most interesting projects I worked on was for a large-scale livestock farming enterprise. The customer faced a common industry challenge: environmental management relied heavily on manual experience. Critical factors—temperature, humidity, ventilation, and cooling—were adjusted largely by human judgment, even though these conditions directly affect animal health, growth performance, and mortality. The customer had accumulated years of operational data but was using very little of it for decision-making, and wanted to apply digitalization and data-driven insight to optimize conditions and reduce livestock losses.

## Livestock Project — Solution & 20% Loss Reduction
We began by connecting manual controls, sensors, PLCs, HMIs, and other field devices into a centralized digital platform, using industrial protocols such as OPC UA and MQTT to collect real-time data on temperature, humidity, ventilation status, and equipment performance. The platform enabled remote monitoring, real-time visualization, centralized management, and automated alerting—so operators could watch conditions remotely and respond quickly when thresholds were reached. For example, when temperature or humidity approached predefined risk levels, the system alerted operators to increase ventilation, activate evaporative cooling, or adjust controls, and it also enabled remote control of certain equipment, reducing response time and improving consistency. After deployment, the customer benchmarked results against historical performance under similar conditions and reported roughly a 20% reduction in livestock losses—a metric drawn from their own operational evaluation and used as a key outcome in the project assessment.

## Livestock Project — My Contribution
My primary responsibility was designing and developing the data-acquisition and application layer: building data-collection pipelines, developing edge-computing applications, creating data-processing workflows, designing visualization dashboards, gathering requirements and communicating with the customer, and supporting deployment and production rollout. In short, my job was to transform raw operational data from field equipment into actionable information the customer could understand and use to improve decisions.

## Low-Cost Data Solutions for SMEs
Many small and medium-sized manufacturers want to pursue digital transformation but lack the budget and IT resources of large enterprises, so cost efficiency was a major design consideration. Our approach reused existing equipment wherever possible, leveraged edge computing to reduce cloud-infrastructure costs, used modular architectures to lower implementation cost, and standardized solution components to minimize custom development. The goal wasn't the most sophisticated system, but the highest business value within a realistic budget—lowering the barrier to entry so smaller manufacturers could start their digital transformation and realize measurable operational benefits.

## Visualization Dashboards — Stack & Business Value
Visualization was a critical component of many projects. On the backend we primarily used Node.js for data processing, integration, and API development; on the frontend we used modern web technologies to build monitoring platforms and operational dashboards. These were used by plant managers, operations teams, and business stakeholders, surfacing metrics such as equipment status, production KPIs, environmental conditions, energy consumption, and alarms/events. The dashboards weren't just monitoring tools—their primary purpose was to support operational decisions. Where decisions had previously relied on experience and manual observation, making real-time data visible let customers quickly spot issues, locate bottlenecks, improve resource utilization, and decide with better information.
